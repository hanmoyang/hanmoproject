Highlight of this month

Click the picture to find out more

“Cindy Sherman: Once Upon a Time”

Classic Shermans in a classy townhouse gallery? You can’t get more museum-quality than that. On view are her society dames, ingenues and art-history icons.
Mnuchin Gallery, Tuesday April 18 2017 - Saturday June 10 2017

“Making Space: Women Artists and Postwar Abstraction”

An international roster of woman artists who worked under a male-dominated midcentury milieu get a turn in the spotlight. Joan Mitchell, Lee Bontecou and Louise Bourgeois are some of the figures celebrated here.
Museum of Modern Art (MoMA), Saturday April 15 2017 - Sunday August 13 2017

“Irving Penn: Centennial”

As one of the giants of 20th-century photography, Irving Penn (1917–2009) was known for his fashion photography, portraits and still lifes. His stunning large format, black-and-white images of models and celebrities helped to define the look of midcentury America. This retrospective mounted on the occasion of Penn’s 100th birthday features some 200 examples of his work, which remains as indelible now as when he first began to create it more than 60 years ago.
The Metropolitan Museum of Art, Thursday April 20 2017 - Sunday July 30 2017

“John Baldessari: Paintings 1966-68”

Working during the mid-1960s in relative isolation, Baldessari created these text and captioned-photo “paintings,” which influenced many the conceptual-oriented artists tover the next two decades—including members of the Pictures Generation.
Craig F. Starr Gallery, Until Saturday May 20 2017
